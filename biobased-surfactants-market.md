<p><p>The biobased surfactants market comprises four main types: anionic, cationic, nonionic, and amphoteric. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge, providing excellent cleansing and foaming properties, often used in detergents. Cationic surfactants, with a positive charge, act as conditioners and antimicrobial agents, common in personal care products. Nonionic surfactants lack charge, offering mildness and versatility, making them popular in industrial applications. Amphoteric surfactants can switch between positive and negative charges, allowing for diverse applications in various formulations.</p></p>
